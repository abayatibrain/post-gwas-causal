# ADR-0001: Wakefield ABF as the primary finemapper, with a simplified SuSiE alongside

Date: 2026-06-17
Status: Accepted

## Context

Fine-mapping has to turn per-SNP association statistics plus LD into a posterior
over which variant(s) are causal. Two families dominate the field:

- **Single-causal-variant ABF** (Wakefield 2009): assumes exactly one causal
  variant per locus, computes a closed-form approximate Bayes factor per SNP,
  and reads off PIPs and a credible set. No LD matrix is even required for the
  PIPs themselves.
- **Multi-causal Bayesian variable selection** (SuSiE, FINEMAP): models several
  causal variants and the LD between them, returning one credible set per
  signal. More faithful to real loci, but iterative and LD-sensitive.

The package needs a *correct, dependency-light, testable* default, plus a path
to multiple signals.

## Options considered

### Option A — Wakefield ABF only
- **Pros:** exact, closed-form, trivially correct, fast; the single-causal
  assumption is reasonable for many loci; easy known-answer tests.
- **Cons:** cannot resolve two independent causal variants at one locus.

### Option B — Full SuSiE / FINEMAP reimplementation
- **Pros:** handles multiple signals; field-leading.
- **Cons:** full SuSiE involves ELBO optimisation, residual-variance
  estimation, and careful handling of in-sample-vs-reference LD mismatch — a
  large surface to get exactly right and to test. Overkill for a portfolio
  toolkit's *primary* path.

### Option C — Wakefield ABF primary + a simplified SuSiE-RSS
- **Pros:** ABF gives a rock-solid, exactly-tested default; a compact
  IBSS-based SuSiE-RSS adds genuine multi-signal capability (sum of single
  effects, posterior assignment `alpha`, purity-filtered credible sets) without
  the full ELBO machinery. Both are validated against `susieR`/standard results
  via the R bridge.

## Decision

**Option C.** `finemap/abf.py` (Wakefield) is the primary, always-correct
finemapper. `finemap/susie.py` provides a working simplified SuSiE-RSS
(IBSS, fixed residual variance) for multi-causal loci.

## Consequences

- The single-causal default is exact and exhaustively unit-tested.
- The SuSiE-RSS holds residual variance fixed and uses a max-`alpha`-change
  convergence surrogate rather than the full ELBO; this is documented in the
  module and flagged as the v0.2.0 hardening target. Its multi-causal recovery
  is tested on a two-causal-variant simulation.
- Both methods are cross-checked against R `susieR` through `rbridge.py`.
